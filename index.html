Shri. Narendra Modi

A visionary Indian prime Minister

Modi's Timeline- Journey of a Comman Man

  • Full name- Narendra Damodar Das Modi.
  • 17th1950- Born in Vadnagar, Mehsana district, Bombay State (present-day Gujrat).
  • As a child Modi helped his father sell tea at vadnagar railway station
  • 1967- completed his higher secondry education
  • When Eight years old Modi joined RSS(Rastriya swayamsevak sangh)
  • In June 1975- Modi was appointed general secretary of the "Gujarat Lok Sangharsh Samiti", an RSS committee coordinating opposition to the Emergency in Gujarat
  • 1990- Modi helped L.K advani and Manohar Joshi organise Ekta Yatra(Journey of Unity) and was named as a member of BJP's National Election Committee
  • November 1995- Modi was elected BJP's National Secretary
  • 3 October 2001- Became the Chief Minister of Gujrat
  • Feb 2002 Gujrat Riots took place under his leadership
  • Modi was elected as Gujrat chief Minister Four Times in a row
  • 26 May 2014- Modi was sworn in as the 14thPrime Minister of India
  • 3 oct, 2014- He started a monthly radio programme titled "Mann Ki Baat"
  • Modi also launched the Digital India programme to promote digital literacy
  • 9 Nov,2016- the government demonetised ₹500 and ₹1000 banknotes, in order to curb corruption and black money
  • 30 may, 2019- He was again sworn in as Prime minister.

Awards and Recognitions

  • Modi was named the Best Chief Minister in a 2007 nationwide survey by India Today
  • Modi was named the Best Chief Minister in a 2007 nationwide survey by India Today
  • he was named one of Time magazine's 100 Most Influential People in the World
  • Forbes Magazine ranked him the 15th-Most-Powerful Person in the World in 2014 and the 9th-Most-Powerful Person in the World in 2015, 2016 and 2018
  • In 2015 he was named one of Time's "30 Most Influential People on the Internet" as the second-most-followed politician on Twitter and Facebook
  • Modi received UN's highest environmental award, the 'Champions of the Earth'
  • He was conferred the 2018 Seoul Peace Prize in recognition of his dedication to improving international cooperation
